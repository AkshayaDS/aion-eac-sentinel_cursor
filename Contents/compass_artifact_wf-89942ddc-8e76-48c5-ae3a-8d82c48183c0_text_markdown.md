### RTX's pain points create urgent AI platform demand

**Pratt & Whitney GTF engine crisis** is the most acute challenge. Contaminated powdered metal in high-pressure turbine disks (manufactured October 2015–September 2021) has created a multi-billion-dollar quality crisis. As of October 2025, **835 aircraft are grounded globally**—32% of the total GTF-powered fleet—versus just 3% for competing CFM Leap engines. Inspection turnaround runs **250-300 days** (versus 60-day expectations). Total cost estimates reach **$6-7B** across RTX and risk-sharing partners, with the initial $3B charge in September 2023 expanding to $5.4B. Powder metal compensation impacts for 2025 were $1.1-1.3B, declining to ~$700M in 2026. This crisis directly demands AI-powered quality inspection, predictive manufacturing analytics, and supply chain optimization.

**Supply chain complexity** spans **14,000+ suppliers** providing parts including critical materials (cobalt, tantalum, chromium, rhenium, nickel, titanium) often from foreign single-source suppliers. Key bottlenecks include structural castings, isothermal forgings, microelectronics, and heat exchangers. **2025 tariff exposure is estimated at $850M.** RTX BBN won an **$8M DARPA contract** for Resilient Supply-and-Demand Networks using AI to predict supply chain shocks.

**Defense backlog conversion** at $268B requires unprecedented production scaling. RTX invested over **$600M in manufacturing expansion in 2025**, including $300M at Raytheon's Redstone missile facility to boost capacity by 50%.

**Workforce challenges** include security clearance processing times of **8-24 months**, a 7.1% aerospace attrition rate (up from 4.3% in 2017), and critical skills gaps in AI, ML, cybersecurity, and embedded software. A May 2025 Pratt & Whitney worker strike temporarily slowed production.

**ITAR/CMMC compliance** is a make-or-break requirement. RTX paid **$200M in August 2024** for 750+ ITAR violations and accumulated **>$950M in combined penalties** for legacy bribery, defective pricing, and export violations. CMMC Phase 2 (November 2026) requires third-party Level 2 certification for CUI-handling contracts. Any Enterprise AI OS must operate within FedRAMP High or air-gapped environments, restrict all data access to U.S. persons, and support NIST SP 800-171's 110 controls.

**Manufacturing and OT/IoT**: RTX's **Connected Factory Initiative** uses lighthouse sites to prove digital technologies before scaling. At the Collins Aerospace Santa Isabel site, AI-enabled automated optical inspection boosted output by **14%**, cut inspection time from 30 to 10 minutes, and halved defect escapes. Next deployment: Real-Time Location Service tracking **10,000 boards** through the factory. RTX is building a **proprietary AI and data platform** integrating commercial tools with cloud computing and advanced analytics.

## TRACK 3: Market context and competitive intelligence

### The DoD just created a $13.4B AI budget line — defense AI is now funded

The FY2026 DoD budget of **$1.01 trillion** includes, for the first time ever, a dedicated **$13.4B allocation for AI and autonomy**—the largest single-year defense AI investment in history. This breaks down into unmanned aerial vehicles ($9.4B), maritime autonomous systems ($1.7B), software and cross-domain integration ($1.2B), underwater capabilities ($734M), autonomous ground vehicles ($210M), and AI/automation technology ($200M). The total DoD IT budget is **$66B**, up $1.8B from FY2025.

The global AI in A&D market stands at **$25.4B (2024)** and is projected to reach **$65.4B by 2034** at 9.91% CAGR. IDC forecasts U.S. A&D spending on AI reaching **$5.8B by 2029**, 3.5x 2025 levels. The CDAO awarded **$200M in contracts** to xAI, OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic in July 2025 for frontier AI capabilities and established the **AI Rapid Capabilities Cell** with $100M allocated across FY2024-FY2025.

A critical new development: **FY2026 NDAA Section 1513** directs DoD to develop a framework addressing cybersecurity and physical security of AI/ML technologies, to be incorporated into **both DFARS and CMMC**. This framework covers source code, model weights, algorithms, training data, and all software used to develop AI. A status update to Congress is due by **June 16, 2026**. Contractors who proactively comply will have a significant advantage.

### ITAR and CMMC create a compliance moat that favors prepared bidders

ITAR requires all defense-related data to be accessible **only by U.S. persons**, with penalties up to **$1M per violation and 20 years imprisonment**. For AI, this means: no commercial AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.) for ITAR data; models must run on FedRAMP High or air-gapped infrastructure; model weights trained on ITAR data are themselves export-controlled; and all logs, cached responses, and audit trails must remain within compliant boundaries. Approved deployment models include AWS GovCloud (IL 2/4/5), Microsoft Azure GCC High, on-premises air-gapped systems, and emerging solutions like TrueFoundry's ITAR-compliant AI Gateway.

CMMC 2.0's DFARS rule became effective **November 10, 2025**, with Phase 2 requiring **C3PAO third-party certification by November 2026** for most Level 2 contracts. Over **200,000 A&D suppliers must comply**, with ~80,000 organizations requiring Level 2. Compliance costs range from $75,000-$300,000 for Level 2 C3PAO certification. The CSA AI Controls Matrix (July 2025) provides **243 control objectives across 18 domains** specifically for AI security.

For the RFP response, HCL's **7 ITAR-compliant U.S. centers**, Butler Aerospace security clearances, AI Force's on-premises deployment capability, and built-in GRC module with PII/toxicity/bias scanners create a compliant-by-design architecture that directly addresses RTX's $200M ITAR penalty history.
